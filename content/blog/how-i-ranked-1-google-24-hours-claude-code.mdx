<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-6">
  The 4-Hour Sprint That Changed Everything
</h2>

<p class="mb-6">
  Let me analyze James's exact process and what we can learn from it:
</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4">
  Lesson 1: Keywords Don't Need to Be Complicated
</h3>

<p class="mb-6">
  James's approach was dead simple:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-6 space-y-2">
  <li>Open ChatGPT</li>
  <li>Ask for 25-50 keywords for your business</li>
  <li>Focus on "high-intent" keywords (where people are ready to buy)</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
  No expensive tools. No analysis paralysis. Just ask AI and move on.
</p>

<p class="mb-6 font-semibold">
  What this means for you: Stop overthinking keyword research. Your customers are searching for simple things like "[your service] near me" or "emergency [your service] [your city]". That's where the money is.
</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4">
  Lesson 2: AI Can Do Your Technical SEO in Minutes
</h3>

<p class="mb-6">
  This blew my mind. James told Claude Code to audit his site and find all SEO issues. Then he literally just said "fix them."
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
  Claude:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-6 space-y-2">
  <li>Created robots.txt files</li>
  <li>Built XML sitemaps</li>
  <li>Optimized images</li>
  <li>Added schema markup</li>
  <li>Fixed meta descriptions</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
  What agencies charge $5,000 for, Claude did in 10 minutes.
</p>

<p class="mb-6 font-semibold">
  What this means for you: Technical SEO isn't some dark art anymore. The barriers that kept small businesses from competing online just disappeared.
</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4">
  Lesson 3: Local Content Is Your Secret Weapon
</h3>

<p class="mb-6">
  Here's where James got smart. Instead of one generic "We serve Charlotte" page, he created 50+ ultra-specific location pages.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
  Each page included:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-6 space-y-2">
  <li>Local landmarks</li>
  <li>Area-specific problems (Charlotte = NASCAR shipping routes)</li>
  <li>Neighborhood details</li>
  <li>Local frequently asked questions</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
  His competitors? One boring "Service Areas" page with a list of cities.
</p>

<p class="mb-6 font-semibold">
  What this means for you: Your customers want to know you understand THEIR specific area. A plumber in Dilworth should have different content than a plumber in Plaza Midwood. This specificity is how you win.
</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4">
  Lesson 4: Speed Wins (Especially on Mobile)
</h3>

<p class="mb-6">
  James made a brilliant point: When a trucker is broken down on I-77 with spotty service, your site better load FAST.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
  He had Claude optimize everything:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc list-inside mb-6 space-y-2">
  <li>Compressed images</li>
  <li>Converted to WebP format</li>
  <li>Got load time under 2 seconds</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
  Then he used Google PageSpeed Insights to find issues and literally copy-pasted the errors to Claude to fix.
</p>

<p class="mb-6 font-semibold">
  What this means for you: Your customers are searching on phones. In parking lots. With bad service. If your site takes 10 seconds to load, you've already lost them to your competitor.
</p>
