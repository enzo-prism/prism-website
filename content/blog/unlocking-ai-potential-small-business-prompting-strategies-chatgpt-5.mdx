<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-6">Evidence-Based Prompting Techniques Tailored for Small Businesses</h2>

<p class="mb-6">
The video distills prompting down to a handful of high-impact, easy-to-remember strategies that shine with reasoning models. We've selected our favorites based on their practicality and tested effectiveness, adapting them for real-world small business scenarios.
</p>

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Generate Multiple Options and Check for Consistency (Self-Consistency Tip)</strong><br />
    Instead of accepting the first AI response, ask the model to produce several variations and evaluate them for alignment. This reduces hallucinations (AI's made-up facts) and ensures reliable outputs.<br />
    <em>Business Application:</em> When brainstorming marketing ideas for your e-commerce store, prompt: "Give me five different social media campaign ideas for promoting our new product line, then check them for consistency in branding and feasibility." Use this with ChatGPT-5 to refine concepts quickly—our clients have used it to create consistent customer service scripts that align with their voice, cutting revision time in half.
  </li>
  
  <li>
    <strong>Use Tools Like Code or Math for Problem-Solving (Program of Thought)</strong><br />
    Encourage the AI to "program" its thinking by writing code or using math, making it far more accurate for numerical or logical tasks.<br />
    <em>Business Application:</em> For inventory management, try: "Write a simple Python function to calculate optimal reorder points based on these sales data points [insert data], then explain the results." This is gold for small retailers or service providers analyzing expenses. Prism clients in logistics have leveraged this to automate budgeting, spotting cost-saving opportunities that basic prompts miss.
  </li>
  
  <li>
    <strong>Plan First, Then Execute (Plan and Solve)</strong><br />
    Have the AI outline a step-by-step plan before diving into the task, allowing you to critique and refine early.<br />
    <em>Business Application:</em> Planning a product launch? Prompt: "Create a step-by-step plan for launching our new service, including timelines and resources, then execute the first three steps." This mirrors how we at Prism help clients map out growth strategies—it's especially useful for solopreneurs juggling tasks, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.
  </li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
These techniques aren't about memorizing endless guides; they're straightforward ways to engineer better AI interactions. The video backs them with research from Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI, showing measurable improvements in accuracy and creativity.

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-6">Structural Principles to Build Robust Prompts</h2>

<p class="mb-6">
Beyond specific tips, the video highlights foundational "structures" for any prompt, which we've found transformative for small businesses dealing with unpredictable AI outputs.
</p>

<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li><strong>Add Guardrails and Edge Cases:</strong> Don't just describe the ideal scenario—specify what to do if things go wrong. For example, "If data is incomplete, ask for clarification before proceeding." This prevents off-track responses in business-critical tasks like financial forecasting.</li>
  
  <li><strong>Position Key Info Strategically:</strong> Put critical instructions in the first 10% and reiterate constraints at the end. Attention fades in the middle, so sandwich examples or data there.</li>
  
  <li><strong>Prioritize Negative Examples:</strong> Show what <em>not</em> to do more than what to do. E.g., "Avoid generic phrases like 'in conclusion' in email drafts—keep it professional and concise." This refines outputs for branding consistency.</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
In our analysis, these principles make prompts "bulletproof," especially for small teams without dedicated AI experts. A Prism client in consulting used them to generate client reports that were 20% more polished, impressing prospects and closing deals faster.
</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-6">The Power of Meta-Prompting: Let AI Help You Prompt Better</h2>

<p class="mb-6">
Our favorite insight from the video? Models like ChatGPT-5 know themselves best—use "meta-prompting" to have them refine your queries. This creates a self-improvement loop that's perfect for busy owners.
</p>

<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-2">
  <li><strong>Self-Improvement Loop:</strong> "Here's my prompt—how would you improve it for better results from you?"</li>
  <li><strong>Uncertainty Check:</strong> "What parts of this request are unclear? What assumptions are you making?"</li>
  <li><strong>Capability Discovery:</strong> "How would you approach this with no constraints? What tools would help?"</li>
</ul>

<p class="mb-6">
Apply this to business: Before finalizing a content strategy, meta-prompt ChatGPT-5 to optimize your query. It uncovers hidden efficiencies, like suggesting data integrations you hadn't considered. We've guided Prism clients through this for customer analysis, turning vague ideas into actionable insights.
</p>
