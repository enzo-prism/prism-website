## The short version

<p class="mb-6">
Four things stand out this morning. Notion made AI more useful for revenue teams by connecting Salesforce search directly into the workspace. GitHub pushed Copilot further into true agentic execution for JetBrains users. Figma turned AI usage into something design leaders can actually budget and manage. Microsoft kept reinforcing the idea that the next real AI advantage is not one more assistant, but a better operating model for agents, approvals, and trust.
</p>

## 1. Notion is getting more useful for founder led sales and pipeline work

<p class="mb-6">
On March 11, Notion added AI search across Salesforce accounts, leads, opportunities, and contacts directly inside Notion. That means a founder or operator can ask for pipeline context, deal history, or contact summaries without bouncing between tools.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
Source: <a href="https://www.notion.com/releases" class="font-semibold underline underline-offset-4">Notion What’s New</a>
</p>

### Why this matters for founders

<p class="mb-6">
Small teams lose speed when customer context lives in too many places. If your revenue meetings happen in Notion but your pipeline lives in Salesforce, AI can now bridge that gap more directly. That cuts prep time and helps leadership make faster decisions with less manual digging.
</p>

### How to use it this week

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li>Create one shared Notion page for weekly pipeline review.</li>
  <li>Use Notion AI to pull summaries for your top open deals, stalled opportunities, and at risk accounts.</li>
  <li>Turn those answers into three next actions only, such as follow up, reprioritize, or escalate.</li>
  <li>Compare how long the review takes before and after the AI assisted workflow.</li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
If you want AI to help sales, this is the kind of grounded integration that actually changes execution.
</p>

## 2. GitHub Copilot is getting more agentic for real product teams

<p class="mb-6">
GitHub’s March 11 update for JetBrains IDEs pushed custom agents, sub agents, and plan agent into general availability, while also adding hooks in preview and auto approve support for MCP. The important point is not the IDE brand. It is that GitHub is making it easier for teams to shape how AI agents work inside their real development environment.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
Source: <a href="https://github.blog/changelog/2026-03-11-major-agentic-capabilities-improvements-in-github-copilot-for-jetbrains-ides/" class="font-semibold underline underline-offset-4">GitHub Copilot JetBrains update</a>
</p>

### Why this matters for founders

<p class="mb-6">
If you run a software enabled business, your leverage comes from getting more shipped with the same team. Custom agents and workflow hooks move AI beyond autocomplete and toward repeatable execution. That helps smaller product teams standardize how code gets planned, checked, and handed back for review.
</p>

### How to use it this week

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li>Choose one engineering task that repeats every sprint, such as writing tests, triaging bugs, or scaffolding internal tools.</li>
  <li>Define a lightweight agent instruction set for that task.</li>
  <li>Add one hook or review rule so the agent cannot bypass your quality bar.</li>
  <li>Measure whether the team ships that task faster with fewer review rounds.</li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
This is how founders turn AI from a personal productivity toy into a team level shipping advantage.
</p>

## 3. Figma is making AI spend easier to manage like a real operating budget

<p class="mb-6">
Figma’s AI credit update is becoming real this week. Teams can now track AI credit usage in the billing dashboard, and as of March 11, 2026, Figma is introducing both a shared AI credits subscription and pay as you go billing for extra AI usage.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
Source: <a href="https://www.figma.com/blog/updates-to-ai-credits-in-figma/" class="font-semibold underline underline-offset-4">Updates to AI credits in Figma</a>
</p>

### Why this matters for founders

<p class="mb-6">
One of the hidden problems in AI adoption is cost fog. Teams experiment, credits burn down, and nobody really knows which workflows are creating value. Figma is moving AI usage closer to normal budget management. That is a strong pattern for founders to copy across their broader AI stack.
</p>

### How to use it this week

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li>List every paid AI tool your creative or product team touches.</li>
  <li>Separate exploration spend from production spend.</li>
  <li>Assign one owner to review usage and value every Friday.</li>
  <li>Keep funding the workflows that shorten cycle time or improve conversion assets, and cut the rest.</li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
The founders who win with AI are rarely the ones with the most tools. They are the ones with the clearest spend discipline.
</p>

## 4. Microsoft keeps reinforcing that founders need an AI operating model, not random tools

<p class="mb-6">
Microsoft’s March 9 Frontier Suite announcement is still one of the clearest market signals from the week. The company is packaging Copilot, Agent 365, model choice, and governance into one story built around intelligence plus trust. Whether or not you buy Microsoft, the strategic signal matters.
</p>

<p class="mb-6">
Source: <a href="https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/03/09/introducing-the-first-frontier-suite-built-on-intelligence-trust/" class="font-semibold underline underline-offset-4">Introducing the First Frontier Suite built on Intelligence + Trust</a>
</p>

### Why this matters for founders

<p class="mb-6">
Most small companies still use AI like a loose collection of chats and subscriptions. Microsoft is arguing that the next step is system design. That means deciding where context lives, where agents act, who approves high risk output, and how security and auditability stay intact as usage grows.
</p>

### How to use it this week

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li>Pick one cross functional workflow, such as lead follow up, proposal writing, customer onboarding, or support escalation.</li>
  <li>Break it into context gathering, draft execution, approval, and system update.</li>
  <li>Assign AI to the first two steps only where the error cost is low.</li>
  <li>Keep approval and final system changes human until the process earns more trust.</li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
That gives you a practical AI operating model instead of scattered experimentation.
</p>

## What founders should do next

<p class="mb-6">
The best move today is not to chase a brand new model. It is to tighten the workflows around the tools you already have.
</p>

<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-4">
  <li>Connect AI to a system of record, such as Salesforce, your codebase, or your design backlog.</li>
  <li>Put a simple budget and review rhythm around AI usage.</li>
  <li>Decide where agents can act on their own and where a human still needs final control.</li>
  <li>Track one output metric, not ten, so you know whether the workflow is actually getting better.</li>
</ol>

<p class="mb-6">
That is how a founder turns a quiet weekend in AI news into a stronger business on Monday.
</p>
