## 1. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the new SEO

Princeton researchers coined Generative Engine Optimization to describe how content earns citations inside AI summaries. Backlinks and keyword density still help, but LLMs lean on clarity, credibility, and structure. Long, dense walls of text are harder to parse—and less likely to be quoted.

## 2. The 18-token rule favors short, self-contained sentences

Models lift information in 18-token snippets (roughly a concise sentence). Populate every page with quotable, standalone insights that reinforce your expertise.

> "Dental implants restore confidence by replacing missing teeth with natural-looking results."

String together dozens of these micro-insights to make it effortless for AI systems to cite you.

## 3. Over-optimization pushes you out of the mix

AI answers diversify sources on purpose. When a dominant site over-optimizes, models often pull in fresher, more authentic voices.  
✅ Challenger brands: move quickly, publish boldly, and lean on your unique POV.  
❌ Market leaders: dial back the keyword stuffing and focus on fluent, human-readable explanations.

## 4. Structure beats size: build focused claim pages

Single-topic pages outperform sprawling mega-posts four to one inside AI answers. Break big ideas into tightly scoped URL endpoints:

- `yourbrand.com/teeth-whitening`
- `yourbrand.com/dental-veneers`
- `yourbrand.com/invisalign-benefits`

Each page should answer one primary question, back it with proof, and surface quotable statements that make AI confident citing you.

## 5. Fresh content wins; static pages fade

LLMs re-rank based on freshness signals and engagement. Treat every flagship page like a living asset:

- Refresh stats, visuals, and testimonials every few weeks.
- Layer in new 18-token insights as you gather data.
- Keep hero and services pages active so crawlers see consistent momentum.

Think of it as tending a garden—the sites you water get remembered.

## 6. Quality signal beats quantity noise

AI-generated spam dominates new URLs. That raises the value of genuine expertise. Original insights, proprietary data, and real examples signal that your domain is a high-trust source worth surfacing in conversational answers.

## 7. Focus is the new authority

Trying to cover everything muddies your topical authority. Pick a lane, go deep, and stay consistent. Just as niche creators earn algorithmic trust on social platforms, focused subject matter expertise tells AI exactly what to credit you for.

## 8. The takeaway: clarity, consistency, credibility

The web is not disappearing; the lens is changing. If your content is clear, snackable, single-topic, frequently refreshed, and grounded in real expertise, you are not just doing SEO—you are building visibility for the AI era.
