Hormozi, who's built and scaled businesses to nine figures, boils it down: Stop competing on price wars and start dominating with value that screams "yes." </p>

## First, Nail Your Market: Hunt for the "Starving Crowd"

<p>
Hormozi kicks off with a brutal truth: Your offer's only as good as the market it's hitting. He calls it finding a "starving crowd"—folks with massive pain, the cash to fix it, easy to reach, and in a growing space. Health, wealth, relationships? Those are evergreen goldmines because people are desperate.
</p>

<p>
For SMBs, this means ditching the "serve everyone" trap. Say you're a family dentist (one of our fave clients). Don't just offer cleanings—target busy parents who dread kid meltdowns at the dentist, with deep pockets and a Google search history full of "pain-free pediatric dentistry." Narrow it down, and suddenly you're not competing with every clinic; you're <em>the</em> go-to.
</p>

<p>
At Prism, we apply this by digging into your niche during our initial chats. We build websites that speak directly to that starving crowd, optimize your local listings to match the intent, and launch ad campaigns that reinforce the message—think targeted landing pages that highlight pain points like "no more endless wait times" for wellness communities or "seamless online booking" for bike shops. Result? More qualified leads flooding your funnel, not tire-kickers.
</p>

## The Magic Formula: Crack the Value Equation

<p>
Here's where Hormozi gets math-y, but in a good way. He lays out the Value Equation: Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice). In plain English? Amp up what customers dream of getting and how likely they think it'll happen, while slashing the wait time and hassle.
</p>

<p>
Picture this for your startup: Your dream outcome isn't just "a new app"—it's "doubling customer LTV in 90 days." We boost perceived likelihood with testimonials baked into the design, cut time delay by launching MVPs fast, and minimize effort by handling the tech headaches for you.
</p>

<p>
SMBs often flop here by underdelivering on value—generic websites that take forever to build and require you to micromanage. Not us. We design systems where your acquisition funnels (hello, automated email sequences) deliver quick wins, like first leads in week one. Higher value means you can charge premium without blinking, turning one-off clients into loyal fans.
</p>

## Build a "Grand Slam Offer": Stack It Till They Can't Say No

<p>
The book's heart: Crafting a Grand Slam Offer. Steps? ID the dream, list every problem blocking it, brainstorm solutions, deliver 'em in killer ways, then trim the fat and stack the winners.
</p>

<p>
For a software startup we worked with, problems included clunky user interfaces killing conversions and slow load times frustrating early adopters. Solutions? Custom designs that feel intuitive, plus bonuses like A/B testing baked in. We stack it: Core app build + free SEO audit + ongoing tweaks for the first month.
</p>

<p>
This isn't theory—it's how we help SMBs escape commodity hell. Instead of a basic website, we deliver a full growth package: conversion-focused pages, Google Business Profile optimization, and ad campaigns tuned for velocity. Charge more? Absolutely. But the value gap is huge, so founders see ROI fast. Hormozi nails it: Make it an "expensive bargain."
</p>

## Amp It Up: Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, and Guarantees

<p>
Hormozi's enhancers turn good offers nuclear. Scarcity (limited spots), urgency (act now or miss out), bonuses (extras that wow), and guarantees (risk-free trials) create FOMO on steroids.
</p>

<p>
In SMB land, this looks like time-sensitive launches: "Book your design consult this week and get a free app prototype." Or guarantees like "If we don't boost your leads 20% in 60 days, next month's tweaks are on us." We weave these into your funnels—scarcity pop-ups on sites, urgency emails in automations.
</p>

<p>
Why it works? People hate missing out more than spending. For startup teams juggling a million things, these tactics cut through the noise, turning browsers into buyers.
</p>
